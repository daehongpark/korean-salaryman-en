Your Guide to K-Drama Filming Locations in Seoul (as of 2026)

S
Korean Salaryman
June 27, 2026
Read time 37 min
SUMMARY

Exploring K-drama filming locations in Seoul offers a unique, immersive way to experience Korean culture, blending iconic sights with trendy new spots. Plan your trip using local navigation apps and T-Money, keeping in mind that while these locations are exciting, they are also part of everyday life for locals.

📅 Updated 2026.06.27
Key takeaways
  • Exploring K-drama filming locations in Seoul is a popular way for foreigners to experience Korean culture firsthand.
  • Iconic spots like Namsan Tower and Bukchon Hanok Village remain popular, but new, trendier cafes and neighborhoods are constantly emerging as drama backdrops.
  • For the best experience, use local navigation apps, visit on weekdays, and be ready to immerse yourself in the drama's atmosphere, but remember to respect local residents.
👤 This is for readers who are curious about experiencing K-drama culture in Seoul by visiting real-life filming locations and want honest, practical advice from a local.

Lately in Korea, it feels like every other conversation with a foreigner starts with, “Where was that scene from [insert latest hit K-drama here] filmed?” You’ve probably seen the beautiful backdrops on your screen and wondered if they’re real. The short answer is yes, many of those stunning K-drama filming locations in Seoul are actual places you can visit right now in 2026, and experiencing them is a fantastic way to dive deeper into Korean culture. Seoul itself is a massive, living film set, constantly reinventing itself for new stories.

The Evergreen Iconic K-Drama Spots (Still Relevant, Trust Me)

Let’s be honest, some K-drama filming locations in Seoul are like old friends – they just keep showing up, drama after drama. The big ones are still big for a reason. Take Namsan Tower (Namsan Seoul Tower): you’ve seen it in everything from 'Boys Over Flowers' to 'Crash Landing on You'. The love locks, the panoramic city views… it’s a classic for a reason. I’ve probably been up there a dozen times, mostly dragged by visiting relatives or for early-days dates. It costs around 16,000-21,000 won (roughly $12-16 USD) for the observatory, plus the cable car ride, which is another 11,000 won ($8 USD) round trip. Another perennial favorite is Bukchon Hanok Village, with its traditional Korean houses (hanok). It's incredibly picturesque, especially at dawn, but it's also a residential area. My first office was actually nearby, and I’d often see tourists trying to snap photos while residents were just trying to get to work. It's free to wander, but be mindful of the 'quiet village' signs.

Point: These spots are famous for a reason, but be prepared for crowds and remember that real people live there.

Discovering the Fresh, Trendy K-Drama Filming Locations in Seoul

Beyond the classics, K-dramas are always on the hunt for fresh aesthetics, which means new cafes, quirky shops, and entire neighborhoods suddenly become must-visit K-drama filming locations in Seoul. Right now, areas like Seongsu-dong are massive. It used to be an industrial zone, full of shoemaking factories, but now it’s packed with renovated warehouses turned into trendy cafes and pop-up stores. You’ll see it in dramas focusing on younger, artistic characters. One of my junior coworkers, who is totally hooked on the latest rom-com, dragged us to a specific cafe in Seongsu last month, convinced it was where the leads had their first accidental meeting. The coffee was good, but it was packed! These places are often free to enter, but you’ll pay typical cafe prices, around 6,000-8,000 won ($4.50-6 USD) for a coffee. Another emerging hotspot is Yeonnam-dong, known for its quieter, more residential vibe but also its charming, unique eateries and small boutiques. It's often used for scenes requiring a more 'slice-of-life' feel.

Point: The real gems are often in newly gentrified areas; they offer a more current and local flavor of Seoul.

Your Practical Itinerary for Hunting K-Drama Locations

Okay, so you’ve got your list of K-drama filming locations in Seoul. How do you actually get there? First, you need a T-Money card. This is your lifeblood for public transport – subway, bus, even taxis. You can buy and top it up at any convenience store (pyeonijeom) or subway station. A typical subway ride costs around 1,400-1,550 won ($1.10-1.20 USD) depending on distance. Next, navigation is key. Forget Google Maps; it doesn't work well here. You need Naver Maps or KakaoMap. My company's building is notorious for being hard to find, and I use Naver Maps daily just to navigate my commute or find a new restaurant for hoesik (the after-work company dinner). Both apps are available in English and give excellent public transport directions. As for timing, always aim for weekdays, and if possible, mornings. Weekends and public holidays turn even the most obscure locations into tourist traps. If a specific drama cafe is on your list, try to visit right when it opens.

Point: Seamless travel means local apps and off-peak timing; don't rely on what you use back home.

More Than Just Looking: How to Live the Drama Vibe

Simply seeing a spot is one thing, but truly experiencing K-drama filming locations in Seoul is another. Many tourists love to rent hanbok (traditional Korean dress) near palaces like Gyeongbokgung. You can find shops around the palace offering rentals for about 20,000-50,000 won ($15-38 USD) for a few hours. My cousin, who visits from abroad every couple of years, actually did this with his girlfriend last spring; I just sat on a bench and watched, but they had a blast taking photos. It sounds a bit cheesy, but it genuinely adds to the immersion. Beyond dressing up, consider trying the food featured in your favorite drama. If a character was eating tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes) from a street vendor in Myeongdong, go find some. If they were sharing chimaek (fried chicken and beer) with friends, find a local spot. It's about engaging all your senses, not just your eyes. This is where your nunchi (the art of reading the room) comes in handy – observe what the locals are doing, where they're eating, and blend in.

Point: Immerse yourself fully; wear the clothes, eat the food, and don’t be afraid to embrace the drama fantasy.

What Locals Really Think About the K-Drama Location Boom

Honestly, as a local, the K-drama craze has mixed feelings for us. On one hand, it’s undeniably cool to see our city, our culture, and even our everyday lives getting so much global attention. It sparks a sense of national pride, and it’s certainly good for the economy. On the other hand, the surge in tourism can sometimes be a bit much. Areas like Bukchon Hanok Village, which I mentioned earlier, are residential. While it’s beautiful, the sheer volume of tourists constantly taking photos and sometimes being loud can be disruptive to the people who actually live there. My friends and I often joke about avoiding certain areas on weekends because they’ve become too popular thanks to some drama. It’s part of the palli-palli (hurry-hurry, do-it-now mindset) of Korean trends – things blow up fast. So, when you visit these K-drama filming locations in Seoul, please remember to be respectful. You’re visiting someone’s home, someone’s workplace, someone’s favorite cafe. Enjoy the magic, but do it quietly and consciously.

Point: While the global attention is great, the crowds can be a nuisance for locals, so visit respectfully.

Questions people always ask

Q. What are the most famous K-drama filming locations in Seoul?

A. Some of the most famous K-drama filming locations in Seoul include Namsan Tower, Bukchon Hanok Village, Gyeongbokgung Palace, and various trendy cafes and streets in neighborhoods like Seongsu-dong and Hongdae. These spots frequently appear in popular dramas due to their picturesque scenery or unique urban charm.

Q. What apps should I use to find K-drama locations in Seoul?

A. For accurate navigation to K-drama filming locations in Seoul, you should use local Korean apps like Naver Maps or KakaoMap. These apps provide reliable public transportation routes and are available in English, unlike Google Maps which has limited functionality in Korea.

Q. Is it expensive to visit K-drama filming locations in Seoul?

A. Visiting most outdoor K-drama filming locations in Seoul is free, though some attractions like Namsan Tower observatory or palace entrances have fees. Transportation is affordable using a T-Money card, with subway rides costing around 1,400-1,550 won (about $1.10-1.20 USD). Costs mainly depend on how many cafes or paid attractions you visit.

Q. How can I find newly popular K-drama filming locations in Seoul?

A. To find newly popular K-drama filming locations in Seoul, follow K-drama fan communities online, check Korean tourism blogs, or even look at social media trends. Trendy neighborhoods like Seongsu-dong and Yeonnam-dong are frequently featured in recent dramas as they offer fresh, modern backdrops.

Q. What is the best time to visit K-drama filming locations in Seoul?

A. The best time to visit K-drama filming locations in Seoul is during weekdays, especially in the morning. This helps you avoid large crowds that gather on weekends and public holidays, allowing for a more relaxed and enjoyable experience while respecting local residents.
