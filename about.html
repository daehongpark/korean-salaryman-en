WHO I AM
HELLO
I'm a salaryman in Seoul. You can call me Korean Salaryman.
I won't tell you my real name, where I work, or what I earn — and honestly, none of that is the point. I'm an ordinary office worker: I take the packed subway every morning, sit through the same meetings you'd recognize anywhere, and grab dinner with coworkers after a long day.

What makes me a little different is that I write all of it down — in English — for people outside Korea who are curious about what this country is actually like beyond the headlines and the highlight reels.
WHY THIS BLOG EXISTS
Most of what the world sees about Korea is the polished version.
K-pop, K-dramas, glossy travel videos — they're real, but they're the surface. Behind them is a country of long commutes, strong coffee, fierce trends that come and go in a week, and small everyday rituals that say a lot about how people here actually think.

I wanted a place that explains the why behind all of it — not as a tourist guide, but as someone who lives it every single day.
WHAT YOU'LL FIND HERE
Three things, told honestly and from experience.
What's trending in Korea right now — the apps, foods, slang, and obsessions everyone here is talking about. Real daily life — work, money, apartments, relationships, and the small frustrations and joys of living in Seoul. And the culture behind it all — the context that makes Korea make sense once you understand it.

No sugarcoating, no clickbait. Just Korea, as we actually live it.
